charleshiggie (2032) on 10-Sep-16:
Well after 4.f3 g4xBh4 5. f3 x Ne4 you have this strange position - black has the advantages of two bishops in a fairly open position, the open g file but the disadvantage of the weal, isolated and doubled h pawns. White has the strong e4-d4 pawn centre, and the doubled e pawns are interesting - giving greater central control and white may be able to use the open f file. It is approximately level, with chances for both sides to win if they play well. I think after the retreat, black has a minuscule edge.
