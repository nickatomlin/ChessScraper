archduke_piccolo (2206) on 05-Mar-16:
"When you're in trouble, don't attack, defend." I have to take issue with your mentor, here, Joe. It is often said that the best counter to a flank attack is a counter-blow in the centre. If none is available, counter-action on the other wing might be sufficient. The counter-attack you began with Qxa6 was quite reasonable in the circumstances, and offered prospects of survival at least as good as direct defence would have done. I also suggested a counter-attack in the centre by e4!, which might even have led to an edge for White. Sometimes, all you have is direct or passive defence, in which case, too bad. But you should always be on the look-out for counter-attack that your opponent, heavily committed to his own assault, is ill-placed to meet. This game might well have afforded a fine example of the type. ********* On the whole, I think you can take something away from this game, Joe. You were fine up to move 18, and still in it with a reasonable chance until the disaster at move 20.
cplusplus11 (1704) on 05-Mar-16:
I agree with archduke_piccolo that "When you're in trouble, don't attack, defend" is usually wrong. It depends on the position what to do. You can improve by learning about pawn structures.
