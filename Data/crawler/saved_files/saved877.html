This was a very interesting game between me and Dave. Now, in the past, I have studied GM games and annotations because of Jack's advise to study games played by Masters and Grandmasters. However, doing too much can be harmful. A perfect example was in my last game against Joann. In an annotation by my favorite annotator during a game with Carlson, there were a bunch of variations. One of them is that a piece or pawn is pinned, so you add another attacker, and are able to win it. In that game, however, the opponents K could not move because it was blocked by one of his pieces. By overanalyzing that, I went wrong in my game against Joann because her K was able to move, and when I did, I got caught in a fork, thus, lost a piece. And, things went downhill from there. Well, this is also a perfect example because in GM games, when the opponent's piece is under attack, they often reattack. Of course, when there's no other way out. So, overanalyzing it, I just kept reattacking his Q when he attacked one of my pieces. But, that is what started to lead to my downfall. It is not the move, however, that left, but it is where I started to go wrong. So, how did it all go down?
