jkarp (1319) on 13-Dec-15:
I've been told that, although nothing wrong with it, when up against a stronger player, castling should, in general, be delayed until everything is developed. Of course, there are exceptions, like if we played the main lines of the Ruy Lopez. 1.e4, e5 2.Nf3, Nc3 3.Bb5, a6 4.Ba4, Nf6, and now 5.0-0 But, otherwise, it's better to hold off because more experienced know what to do and can, generally, get better center control.
damalfi (1742) on 14-Dec-15:
Castling is not "bad", of course. Before it, you must check that nothing more important needs to be done defensively, and you must consider your attack plans, remembering that often attacks are done not because you plan to "crush" your opponent, but you want to "control" him, limiting his moves to the ones that meet your attack. IF none of this happens, to castle is ok. In this case, black has nothing but simply developing moves, and white can well take the tempo to castle. The only downside (meaning "potentially dangerous moves", but not inmediately forcing) could be (maybe) Bh6, but it is dubious.
archduke_piccolo (2206) on 14-Dec-15:
With this move, White is probably committed to playing a Closed type of Sicilian.
