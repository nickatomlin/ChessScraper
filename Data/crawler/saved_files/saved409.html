This was an interesting game between me and Bernard, which I thought was Bernnie. It was also an interesting and unusual week at the chess club. First, we had a new member who I've heard as Jack Stockel has annotated his wins. His name is Stanley who played and defeated Ted. Bernard likes playing me because he wins. Likewise, I enjoy playing him because I lose, and when that happens, I learn. Second, Jack had to leave early as something happened with his wife. Third, Stanley couldn't stay the entire, and my opponent also had to leave early because of a lunch game. With all the strange things happening, I got distracted that it made me forget about the other part of Ken's first lesson. The first one was when to exchange which hasn't gotten any better. I've still been making bad choices, and some aren't going to be annotated. But, deciding to exchange when not knowing what to do has still cost me games. And, refusing to exchange against this opponent last week is what also became costly. So, I wound up messaging IM Natalia Pogonina and GM Susan Polgar, still waiting for a response of when exactly even exchanges work or not. That was common, but what's unusual is forgetting to calculate to multiple threats. It was problematic until that game. But, with all the distractions that occurred, the problem was back. Not only didn't I calculate, but I did not even consider more then one threat because the distractions became so bad. In this case, they could not be avoided because whenever I ignored them, a different distraction occurred which just kept happening. But, that's what caused me to make some bad decisions which is how I lost this particular game.
