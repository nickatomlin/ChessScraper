archduke_piccolo (2206) on 15-Oct-15:
@jkarp: It is a skewer. The bishop attacks the rook, which, forced to move away, uncovers the bishop's attack on the unprotected a-pawn on the same diagonal. It is the reverse - antithesis if you like - of a pin. Bear in mind, too, that the rook can not drop back to d3 to protect the a-pawn on account of his own intervening pawn at d5! There is a bit of a refinement to this 'skewer' as 46.Ra6 would be met by 46...Bxd5ch.
