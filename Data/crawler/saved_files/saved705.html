archduke_piccolo (2206) on 26-Nov-15:
Theory has indicated as far back as the 1960s that 7.Qd2 is by far the strongest continuation. GM Vassily Smyslov used to play 7.Be2 - a quiet developing move. World Champion A. A. Alekhine suggested 7.Nb3 and 7.Bb5, but neither has been shown to offer White very much. A move suggested by Paul Keres is one that I used to play simply because nobody else played it: 7.Qd3 with the idea of bringing the Q over to g3, and developing the bishop at e2 and castling short.
