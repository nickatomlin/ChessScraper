jkarp (1319) on 23-Oct-15:
Believe it or not, I corrected a student who I've indirectly mentored in her annotations, as I learned about hope chess from cplusplus11 Although Corrine won, she said that she played a move hoping to win something. The idea being that she figured her opponent wouldn't see it, which I believe worked. However, I said that even if it worked that's still hope chess since you're hoping that you're opponent won't see the best move. And, hope chess is self destructive because, if they see the threat, you're worse off. Instead, you want real chess where your absolutely playing your best. The only time it might work is against a student since they'll miss things. But, even then, it's still important to be careful because one oversight can mess up the game. Soon I will annotate a game, where I only did one move that I didn't realize was hope chess, which it was due to an oversight and lost immediately because of that.
