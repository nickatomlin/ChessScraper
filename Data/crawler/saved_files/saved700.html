Applying What You Learn-the Pinning Motif
jkarp (1347) vs. reubencpiplupyay (1320)
Annotated by: jkarp (1319)
Chess opening: Ruy Lopez (C72), modern Steinitz defence, 5.O-O

This was an interesting game between me and Reuben. Believe it or not, I have trained Reuben, in our first and second games. Our third game, however, was rated, and I lost. But, this was our fourth game against each other, as he challenged me to another game. In my first win against redfoxrising, I remembered what I learned from charleshiggie. That happened, when charles annotated his game against brigadecommander, and both wound up with two Q's, adding to a total of four Q's on the board. But, Janet, knew how to continue, and won. Well, I applied what I learned there to promote a pawn. So, we wound up with 3 Q's, but I had the extra one. And, without compensation for that, I forced a win, shortly after. Well, in this game, I remembered and visulaized what went wrong against Mr. Stockel where I overlooked how dangerous his pin was. It was a pinning motif, where two of my pawns were pinned, but I could've broken that by moving my monarch into the corner. Failing to do so led to my downfall. Well, I applied what I learned from that game to get this pinning motif against Reuben. When black overlooked it's dangers, I was able to force a win. So, how did this game unfold?

 Trying to get an open h file. I figured it out because I considered capturing his LSB, at once. But, then, as usual, I asked myself why Reuben gave it away and if he blundered, or not. I did a ton of Homework, involving both visualization and analyze the board. By studying this, I figured out that this was not a blunder, after all. The idea being is that I'd lost a pawn, or get mated. The idea being is that 12.hxg3, 12...hxg3, now he has a threat against me, one way or another. If I move, my Nf3, he can immediately play 13...Qh5, which would guarantee a mate in one with 14...Qh2#, or 14...Qh1# And, if I give up my N, and respond with 14.Qxf3, or 14.gxf3, he'll answer with 14...Qh3, resulting in the same thing since my Nf3 is no longer around. I remember from previous mistakes, but espeically from a loss against my OTB coach, as well as, the most recent mistake against Dave. With Jack, he gave away a pawn with how much more offenders he had. So, when I got greedy, he was able to trap and win my Q. Same story with Dave, but it gets worse. I took a pawn, and with a discovered check, he was able to get my Q. Later on, he my K was exposed, and I couldn't castle due to a discovered check by castling Q side. Later on, I realized what my position was like and that he, likely, had a mating sequence, forcing me to resign, in less then 15 moves. Knowing that, since Jack accurately said that I didn't take enough time to study, this time, I was really going to do my Homework.
