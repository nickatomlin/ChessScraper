archduke_piccolo (2206) on 28-Jul-15:
@jkarp: observe that the White d-pawn is supported by the White c-pawn. Black's move ...b5 strikes at the supporting pawn. It has - or is intended to have - an effect similar to knocking out the pit props of a subterranean coal mine, causing it to collapse. Hence: 'undermining'. 'Exacerbate' means 'to make worse', or can even mean simply to irritate or annoy. One might say here that by undermining the d-pawn's supports, Black seeks to exacerbate the precariousness of that pawn's situation.
