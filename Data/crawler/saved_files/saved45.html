When the Dutch cavalry took over...
Dr. Max Euwe vs. Aleksander Alekhine
Annotated by: fiercequeen (1200)
Chess opening: Queen's pawn (A40), Keres defence
Interactive ? Show all comments ? All annotated games ? View chessboard as:
Pages: 12Next »
Needing a new opponent for his next title defense (and still unwilling to play Capablanca again), and with Bogolubov no longer a credible challenger in anyone's eyes, Alexander Alekhine picked a man who had lost two matches to Bogolubov, Max Euwe: National Chess Champion of The Netherlands for many years. If Alekhine picked Euwe as another safe opponent, his plans went awry, owing to a combination of Alekhine's overconfidence, and the fact that by 1935, Max Euwe was a good deal stronger a player than anyone realized... The result was, that Dr. Max Euwe - a math teacher at a maiden school - was to become the 5th ruling World Champion of Chess; until 1937, when Alekhine gained the title back: after a lot of soul searching, no doubt...
