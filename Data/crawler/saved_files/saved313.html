archduke_piccolo (2206) on 09-Oct-16:
The best (re)capture in my view. Taking with the knight would have led to the exchange of knights and possibly the queens as well. That would have made the isolated d-pawn more of a liability than it is with those pieces remaining on the board. At the cost of the strategically problematic d-pawn, Black gets quick development and attacking chances behind the lines. That d-pawn is not a complete liability at that: controlling the useful e4 and c4 squares, and providing just enough cover for Black's mobilization. I'd marginally - but only marginally - prefer Black here.
damalfi (1742) on 09-Oct-16:
