This is the first time in a month that I could make it to the chess club. I went away on the last Friday of August for a long weekend. Then, I had to work at the same time as the chess club, so I thought I'd be competing in the tourney right away, when I got back, or it might be too late. But, what I didn't realize is that others couldn't make it. So, we did not start on Friday, but it will resume next Friday, so this will be the last OTB game that I'll be able to annotate for a while (until the tourney is over because of the absolute time controls there.) And, it's the first time in several months that I got to play against the runner, Jack Stockel which makes it special. This was similar to my rated game with bhidragon because it was, as noted, a while since I could make it. Therefore, like with that game, there was not going to be, "back and forth analysis." So, it was going to be more like a rated then that of a training game because Jack Stockel, similarly, also wanted to see how I was playing these days. By that, I'm not even alluding to recently, but rather, nowdays, so from the present. This time, I played better then average and did better then my game against bhidragon. In fact, he made a terrible mistake that lost his Rook. But, I blew it in the end, and like with that game, failed to figure out what the board was trying to tell me which is how I lost the game. Usually, I don't call it a WTW game because they don't follow the rules. Instead, we both agree on what commentators on what others had been telling me which is that it makes sense to resign when you feel that there is nothing left to learn, after losing material. This game, however, ended with a mate which is why it'll posted in the WTW Annotations thread. It was a different error that got me mated, but before that, the game was lost because with best play, I would've lost a piece without compensation since it happened in the endgame. So, how did it all unravel?

nuntar (1700) on 19-Sep-16:
A shift in meaning appears to have taken place over the last decade or so -- people now use "bad bishop" to refer to the weaker of the two, even if it's not what was traditionally called a bad bishop, i.e. one blocked by its own side's pawns. I think this is a shame, since it leaves us without a word for a useful concept. Here, Black's g4 bishop was by no means bad -- Black may have four pawns on light squares but they are all at the back and don't interrupt the bishop's free rein over the board.
