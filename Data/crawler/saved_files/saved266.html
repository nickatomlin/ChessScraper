 This was a game I won in my reti mini-tournament. The reti opening is named after Richard Reti and he was one of the top players during 1910s and 1920s. Born 28 May 1889 in Pezinok (Austrian-Hungarian) and died 6 June 1929. In New York 1924 Richard Reti played a game against Raul Capablanca which he won. This was Capablanca's only defeat in 8 years and also the first defeat since becoming world champion. https://www.chess.com/article/view/richard-rti

 The reti opening starts with Nf3 and white is trying to control the centre without using his 2 centre pawns. I set up the mini tournament with 1.Nf3 d5, 2. c4 and play then proceeds from there. I went with nf6 protecting my d5 pawn.
