Black draws after 147.Kd3 simply by 147...Rgg4, creating a barrier right across the board with the WK on one side and the WQ on the other. To rub it in, Black might instead have played 147...Rg3ch 148.Kd2 Rf2ch 148.Kf1 Rg2. There is nothing more to be done but 149.Qa7ch Kd6 150.Qxf2 Rxf2 151.Kxf2 Draw: bare kings. Note that had the White king played to the 5th rank at move 147, he would have been mated and/or lost the Queen in short order: 147.Kc5?? Rg5ch 148.Kb6 Rb4ch 149.Ka6 Ra4ch etc.
