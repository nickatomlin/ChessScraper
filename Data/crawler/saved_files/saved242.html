 White pushed his pawn to c3. I believe this is a move in the ponziani opening but white would go with Nf3 and then c3. An expected move might have been d4 to attack e5 and take the centre for white.
2 comments
jkarp (1319) on 22-Dec-16:
Yes, 2.d4 is better. But, best is 2.Nf3 which is the most common move. As that's the most analyze by masters and GM's. As then, black can answer with the Petrov Defense, after 2...Nf6 Or he could play 3...Nc6, which can lead to interesting variations. For example, 3.Bc4 is the Guaco Piano, and 3.Bb5 is the Spanish Opening. The problem here is that this is not analyzed by GM's because it doesn't develop or helps white control the center. Plus, his Nb1 is now blocked, as a direct result. And, in reality, we don't want our pieces cramped as that also takes away mobility.
archduke_piccolo (2206) on 25-Dec-16:
I quite like the idea of the Ponziani Opening 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.c3 ... as a 'natural' method of building up the centre. Of course d2-d4 exd4; cxd4 ... is the plan: a nice, broad centre. The problem of 2.c3 is that it is possible for Black at once to strike at e4 by 2...Nf6, taking the game out of White's planned path. The Centre Game (2.d4) is playable, but White makes certain early concessions after 2...exd4 3.Qxd4 Nc6 4.Qe3; or 2...exd4 3.c3 ... offering a gambit (Danish, or similar gambits). If you enjoy combative games, I recommend the gambits! But the Ponziani is not to be sneezed at.
