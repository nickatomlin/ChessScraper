ANNOTATED GAME

1st American Chess Congress -- New York City, NY 1857
T. Lichtenhein vs. Paul Morphy
Annotated by: teardrop34 (2191)
Chess opening: Scotch gambit (C44), Dubois-Reti defence
Interactive ? Show all comments ? All annotated games ? View chessboard as:
One of the most brilliant games of the last century. (And probably one of my top 10 favorite Morphy Games!) It shows just how much talent Morphy really had. ALL the other Masters present during the game labeled Paul's 12th move a mistake. It is actually a very brilliant move, and reminds me greatly of another chess genius, M. Tal. This game is a brilliant example of the "Two-ROOK Sacrifice." (The Anderssen "Immortal Game" was probably the first great example of this technique.) This game has also been savaged by many writers who have greatly disparaged White's play, few have been able to pick apart Black's play. Not going to say I will be successful but will give it a go and let the audience decide how I did. Assistance with this annotation and the analysis of the more difficult aspects of this game comes from US Life Master, A.J. Goldsby

 This has the appearance of being a {halfway} decent move. GM Edmar Mednis writes in a book of his: "A perfectly reasonable move. White wants Black to capture on e4 with his pawn and thus be left with a broken pawn structure on the Queenside. After the routine 11.0-0, Qxe4; Black has, at no cost to himself; the advantage of the Bishop-pair in an open position. He therefore has a very pleasant advantage."
