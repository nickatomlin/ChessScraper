teardrop34 (2191) on 14-Jul-16:
Exchanging this bishop was bad. White's only active piece on the board now is that d4 pawn. Black's situation is better. The king side is open, however, this gives an attack line for the rook to exploit, a potential diagonal for the bishop. Just for hindsight and for other readers though, consider this: Most Tromp players will respond 3.Bf4, not 3.Bh4 (and certainly not Bxf6 like this player did). The idea is that after f3 and e4, should Black take on e4, he ends up in a Blackmar Diemer (Here's a game with such an example of how to dismantle an opponent with the Blackmar Diemer: gameknot.com) where White has an extra move Bf4. 3...g5 is ridiculous against 3.Bf4 unless you intended some type of Basman defense to begin with.
