archduke_piccolo (2206) on 16-Apr-17:
Not so easy an endgame this. There is no reliable method of replacing the Queen's blockade with the King - it would need a checking move by the former. The pure pawn ending with the c-pawn gone is also not reliably winning, despite the passed pawn, as the time taken to force exchanges on c2 can be used by Black to bring forward his King and centre pawns. Possibly White should play 43.g4 now, partly to constrain the Black f-pawns, but also with a view to establishing his own passer on h5. That achieved, White can then look for ways to progress from there.
