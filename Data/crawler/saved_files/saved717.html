The problem is that you haven't got a piece up, so liquidation was forced. Now, liquidation=exchanging pieces when behind. That tends to favor your opponent. The idea being is that with his pieces, he or she wants to eliminate your pieces so that he has more. That's because, in the endgame, whoever has more pieces generally wins because (A): You guide (coordinate) them with the passed pawns to promote. Or, (B): You don't have pawns, but rather, things like a Knight+Bishop or both Bishops with the King. And, you coordinate that with the King (following general endgame techniques) that you'd use with pawns to deliver the checkmate.
