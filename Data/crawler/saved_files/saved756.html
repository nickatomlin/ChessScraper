archduke_piccolo (2206) on 07-Dec-15:
From Black's point of view, openings are often called Defences (This is the Sicilian). From White's point of view, Openings (e.g. King's Pawn Opening), Games (e.g. Scotch Game) or Attacks (e.g. King's Indian Attack). 1...e6 is called the French Defence; 1...g6 the Modern Defence; 1...d6 the Pirc Defence, and so on. There are some gambits Black plays which used to be called 'Counter-Gambits'. An example is one of my favourites: 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 f5!? which used to be called the Greco Counter-Gambit, but is now known as the Latvian Gambit.

nuntar (1700) on 04-Nov-15:
After 1. e4 e5, 2. Bc4 would be fine. The problem with playing it against a Sicilian is that Black hasn't moved the e-pawn yet, so can play ...e6 blocking the bishop's diagonal. This illustrates why the rule of thumb "knights before bishops" is generally good: the king's knight has one good square where it usually goes, but the bishop has more than one, and which is best might not become clear until later. Thus, as jkarp says, 2. Nf3 is a common and good response.
