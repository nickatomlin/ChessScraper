archduke_piccolo (2206) on 29-Mar-17:
The chances of promoting are very remote. I might have taken muy chances in a rook endgame, even though that also should lose: 31.Rd6 Rxc2 32.Rd8ch Kg7 33.Ne8ch Kf8 24.Nf6ch Ke7 25.Re8ch Kxf6 26.Rxe4. Two pawns down in a rook ending is a losing proposition, but White would have some reason to continue his resistance: active rook, and all the pawns are on one side of the board. If somehow White can exchange his h-pawn for Black's g-pawn, he might even hope for a draw. Even a two-nil pawn advantage is often insufficient in rook-and-pawn end games to win if the pawns are split, and given a reasonably favorable defensive position.
