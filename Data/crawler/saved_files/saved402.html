This was another interesting game between Joe and his opponent. Like the last game, Joe played the exact same way and won. And, he gave some good insight into these games because, earlier today, he said, ..."And don't mind my patented "gallows humor"above.Its all my way of illustrating and proving how many "confident" players(even above 1900)are actually overconfident and lack the proper theory to delay,defend and attack against the unusual moves I make.Its my way of illustrating and instructing the unprepared.Way too many players have this mindset of how a game should or "must"proceed in the opening and they have self programmed their mind to follow a set pattern of development to proceed safely.But when someone like me puts a monkey wrench into their preconceived way of thinking,especially if the game is only 10 minutes or less,I find it moderately easy to defuse their way of thinking and short circuit their chess brain thoughts and their game.Kind of like coming up to a well dressed guy wearing an all white suit with well polished groomed nails and placing his hands,arms and white suit sleeves in a barrel filled with axle grease.I think that would throw his whole day off balance before his tea and crumpet party!Get my drift? Unnecessary overextensions with my Reverse alekhine,taking advantage of the holes I develop on purpose,Q sacs that require mainly only simplification to defeat me rather than trying to take a few pawns or going into defensive mode are some of the key factors to thrown the monkey wrench back at me. As you to mentioned,this "pattern"recognition can be good or bad depending upon how you handle the bizarre.Again,expect the unexpected.1.h3 for example is not necessarily a weak opening move.Nor is moving my knights back into their original postions several times in the opening.Yes,books will tell you otherwise(like the negatives of lost tempo),but if one does not know how to take advantage of lost tempo,then my lost tempo moves can have a considerable sting and offer a swift defeat of my opponents.So weakness was turned into strength. I have shown a few ways to escape my unusual moves in the past if you look close enough,but will get into more detail one day when I have the time.Actually,a simple chess program can guide many of you guys to "see the light"in the proper responses to my moves in a post game analysis.I highly suggest it as a learning tool." I agree with him because, as noted, I made the connection during the first year of me joining this website due to those games where I became overly confident and lost in what should've been won games. So, how did Joe win this game?
