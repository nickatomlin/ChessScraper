 Stop a moment and look at my bishops. Neither one has any hope of moving to any square where they'll be useful any time soon. White's e4 pawn in particular completely locks down my e-pawn, my dark-squared bishop and takes the f5 square away from my other bishop. If I'm going to do anything this game, it has to go. hippoinamarsh (1836) on 14-Sep-16:
I don't know whether c6 is a bad move or not, but I think it's easier to play for the f5 break. Something like Ne7, 0-0 and f5, or Nf6, 0-0, Ne8 and f5 would let you undermine White's centre. You have good attacking chances on the kingside. The bishop is not really bad on g7. It's a useful defensive piece, and if the center ever liquidates, it often becomes strong very quickly. www.chessgames.com is an example of the kind of thing I mean.

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 16-Sep-16:
This game was developing in the direction of Q-side operations by White, with K-side counter-action by Black. Such games can be combative and exciting. There is a useful guideline that say: attack the way the pawns are pointing. Black's did have the appearance of pointing towards the K-side! Black's Q-side thrusts have given White targets to aim at, and taken up time better spent developing the K-side. Fine would have been 7...f5! placing White's centre under pressure from both flanks.
