The f2 and f7 squares
jkarp (1316) vs. nairobigal (1148)
Annotated by: jkarp (1319)
Chess opening: Ruy Lopez (C65), Berlin defence, 4.O-O
Interactive ? Show all comments ? All annotated games ? View chessboard as:
This was an interesting game that I played in charleshiggie's Christmas/New Year Tournament 2016. But, what made this game is that I applied what I learned TA's blitz Q sac games. The main moral that I teach to my students from those games is how important the f2 and f7 squares are. That's because as GM Susan Polgar in a course that I took, these two are the weakest squares on the board and must be watched carefully. Otherwise, it could spell disaster. Remembering that, I was use and apply it to my advantage. So, how did it all go down?

 On top of, getting your pieces developed, the other important lesson is to be careful on the f file. The example is why Joe is able to win after sacking his Queen for pawn. One perspective is because he's stronger. But, the main reason is that those sacs usually occur on the f file. And, that is how his opponents become overly confident. So, that is what needs to be watched out for as letting it become target could spell disaster.
