archduke_piccolo (2206) on 17-Aug-17:
I think you are right, Joe: 9...Bd7 would have been the better option. It would not have constituted all that much of a loss of tempo, at that. Now, two considerations could have applied here. One is what I call 'provoke and destroy'. That is, you provoke such a pawn hit, pull back the bishop, and proceed to exploit the weakness(es) induced. For instance, you could then smite that weakness by playing ...Qc8 later on. I don't think it would have been a great idea in this position, but the notion is worth bearing in mind, especially if you can see prospects of K-side attacks of your own. The other is that White is likely to want that h-pawn to move forward again (to h4) anyhow at some point, so the two moves the bishop spends to go one square haven't really cost anything.
