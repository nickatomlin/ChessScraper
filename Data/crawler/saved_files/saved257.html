This is the first time that I have played against Stanley. However, I have heard of him before. The idea being is that Jack Stockel is also a very good annotator, and as noted, is the main reason that I can annotate games. Now, he did annotate one or two games with Tom. But, most of his annotations involved games with Bernard. There were, however, some games that he annotated with Stanley. That was on Friday night, when a tournament was still ongoing. Stanley won those games, and just like with those other games involving Tom and Bernard, Jack showed how this player won those. This was before Stanley came to my club which happened as a direct result of the chess club on Friday night being cancelled due to the lack of attendance. But, this year, we met him. And, on the first day, Stanley played against Ted and won. Moreover, he showed Ted what went wrong, and thus, coached him. So, I've been wanting to play against Stanley for a while. After my game with Perry which I won, when he tried out the Domiano's Defense, it worked out that I got to play against this opponent. The bad part is that there was only 23 minutes, but I was pretty sure that I was going to lose which didn't matter. The real reason for wanting to play him was because of how Stanley coached Ted, and from that, I realized that I could learn from him, as well.. Well, this particular game went quite well, especially when you consider me having only a very limited amount of time before having to get ready. However, I made an error that lost. In doing so, Stanley mated which is why it is a WTW game. And, on top of it, in the aftermath, he coached me by revealing where I went wrong. And, what happened is identical to where I went wrong against bhidragon. In other words, it wasn't the exact same move since every position is different, but the mistake was the same. Therefore, it was the same lesson to be learned. So, how did it all go down?
