Similar to, "The Real Jim Frior's Tournament", in honor of Jim Friar, tactical-abyss showed people some of GM Victor Korchnoi's games, in honor of him. I didn't hear about him until that thread, but he was also considered to be one of the best players in the world. I don't think he was ever in a world's champion tourney. But, this game shows how good he was. That's because, at the time, GM Fischer was still considered the best player in the world due to being where Carlson is today. Except for rare circumstances, Fischer was undefeatable. There were only a few occasions, in which, players could draw against him. And, it was even more rare for someone to defeat him while remaining the world champion, but this was one of those times which of even more signifance. And, according to what I saw, this was very similar to what I annotated with the Traxler Defense, in which none of these players made a mistake, but Fischer lost which for me requires even more analysis then the one that reversion asked to me annotate. So, how did it all go down?
