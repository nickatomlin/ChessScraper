jkarp (1319) on 07-Jul-16:
First, had you continued, it's mate, no matter what you do. Don't get the R involved, and he has 48...Qh2# But, if you play 48.Rf2, then 48...Qxf2 and the monarch is confined to the corner and on the first rank which is similar to what you see in terms of N+B which means that he'd still mate with 49...Qh2# Second, I'd would've resigned a little earlier for the reason noted above, as that's where my resignation would've occurred. Overall, you played very well, especially, given how much of a fight you put up. My overall advice would be to try and figure out everything that the board is revealing from head to toe. Meaning, try to figure out the entire story. And, don't take things for granted. In other words, don't give away anything, if it's avoidable. Just one passed pawn can be enough to become a killer, but he had more then that with how far ahead he was. Also, avoid weakening the K side. So, don't move the B just because it doesn't appear to be blocked by the N since that's what got the B, followed by Q, out of play. And, don't voluntarily expose the K by advancing the pawn right in front of it so many squares. As you saw, he had a lot of attacking forces against the K, right after you allowed the doubled pawns near the K and had played 11.Bh3. But, we're in the process of learning, so take this advice one step at a time. Sooner or later, things are going to become second nature, and you will improve. It's just that these are the difficult things to wrap yourself around which is also the obstacle to overcome.
