 Defending f7. Take a minute and think what you should play. White clearly has a nice position. You could play Qb3 or Qh5 with extra pressure on f7 which is somewhat hard to meet nicely. You could just take on h7 with the knight, for the rook to move, and then repeat the position at material equality. You could just take on f7 anyway, giving two pieces for a pawn and a rook and then play Qh5 at the end to pick up the h7 pawn anyway. Not to mention some Ba3 developing move or e5 trying to smash open the middle. Consider what you'd play and we'll talk about it in a second.
