jkarp (1319) on 17-Nov-15:
?? I understand why you played this quite well. It's because I told you how important it was to develop your pieces. However, that's just part of General Opening Guidlines, which also includes not moving the same piece twice. Now, this is important because there are times where you must deviate from guidelines. This happens if you find a better move that wins something. But, more often, it's when a piece or a pawn is en-prise (being threatened), or mate is also being threatened. In this case, your Ne5 was en-prised. Therefore, it had to retreat. In addition, as I've seen before, if black didn't find the right response, your next move, likely, would've been 6.Nf7, forking his Rh8 and Qd8 (thus) winning his Queen. However, that is Hope Chess because you're hoping that you're opponent will go wrong. On the other hand, Hope Chess is a way to go wrong. It's better not to Hope for anything, as that also turns into unsound aggression or a cheapo tactic, as we see here, but to Play Real Chess. Consequently, by playing this, black is going to win a piece, in return.
