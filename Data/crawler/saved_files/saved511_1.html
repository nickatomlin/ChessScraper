cdeburca (1840) on 07-Apr-16:
When you're losing - as you undoubtedly are here - you shouldn't swap pieces off. 27. Qe4 gave black a chance to swap queens off and make things much easier for himself. Now Qf2 is still a good move, as g3 is now gone. But in principle, don't offer piece swaps when you're losing unless it's absolutely necessary. Meanwhile, note that g3 is now weak because of the h4 push earlier. So all pawn moves have consequences!
jkarp (1319) on 07-Apr-16:

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 10-Apr-16:
Black was right not to exchange queens, even though it is in his interests in principle. A tactical situation has arisen in which keeping the queens on forces the decision more quickly. White can avert immediate mate only at the cost of a rook.
