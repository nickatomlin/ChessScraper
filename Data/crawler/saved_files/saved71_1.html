archduke_piccolo (2206) on 18-Jun-17:
In the game you mention, Joe, you had the exchange lead, and your d-pawns were doubled. Far from being a liability to you (or an asset to your opponent), they were indeed an asset to you, for two reasons: 1. You had the half-open e-file to work with, and 2. you had TWO levers - f- and the rear d-pawn - with which to prise open the e-file. The lead d-pawn kept the enemy d-pawn back, so White had no effective way to prevent your opening up the e-file and invading White's position. Doubled pawns aren't always a liability!
