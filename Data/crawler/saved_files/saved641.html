 This is not a conventional opening. It's understandable, if this were a KG, where you started with 1.e4, e5 2.f4 But, otherwise, it's like the Damiano's Defense. So, you don't want to advance the f pawn so early. That's because, as I learned with TA's blitz Q sac, that I used to teach a student when he opened with that defense, the f file is considered the weakest square on the chess board. If it's moved early in the game, one could win up in trouble. Better was 1.e4, 1.c4, or 1.d4 which are most analyzed by GM's.
