cplusplus11 (1704) on 08-Aug-15:
? Why didn't you take the free pawn? Maybe you missed 7. dxe5. I suggest you should implement a 5 step thought process so you won't omit good moves from the list of move you consider. Step 1 What are the threats caused by the opponent's move? How does he weaken/strengthen his/your position? Step 2 What are some goals or plans you want to achieve? Step 3 What are the candidate moves that MIGHT achieve these goals? Step 4 Which of these moves are safe? Step 5 Which of these safe candidate move is the best?....................................................... I read about this good thought process in "The Improving Chess Thinker" chess book. Here's How I would use the thought process in the position. Step 1 - opponent's moves & threats: black just played 6...bxc6. This does not contain any threats. Step 2 - plan: My plan is to develop and castle, and there are no winning tactics, or to execute the tactic, if one exists. Step 3 - candidate moves: 7. O-O, 7. Bg5, 7. dxe5 (doesn't develop, but MIGHT win, so it's a candidate move). Step 4 - Which are safe: all the moves are safe. Step 5: calculate and find that 7. dxe5 wins a pawn. Then conclude that it is better than the others because it wins a pawn when the others do not. I have proved that 7. dxe5 is the best move, so I play it.
