archduke_piccolo (2206) on 27-Jan-17:
The difference between a good move and a bad one is often simply a matter of timing. Black's plan is plain to see - she wants ...Qh5 and ...Qxh2#. That it is easily contained is not enough to write it off as wishful thinking, as we all know that short-range threats that require a response is an effective way to make progress and keep the initiative. This knight move is simply too soon to be effective, and needed more preparation. In the present situation, there would have been no time for that, so 11...Nxe4 12.Bxe4 Qf6 (say) would have been OK for Black. Bear in mind, exchanges are in Black's interest - in principle - now that she's a piece ahead.
