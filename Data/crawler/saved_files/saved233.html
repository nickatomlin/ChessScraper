Patience is of a Virtue
jkarp (1309) vs. 1pawnsoon2queen (1353)
Annotated by: jkarp (1319)
Chess opening: Pirc defence (B07)
Interactive ? Show all comments ? All annotated games ? View chessboard as:
This was a remarkable team game between me and Ken. And, it is historic, by my books. I've had a lot of training games with some very good coaches and mentors. But, the main problem I had did not get solved until recently. This happened during a training game with God since it was in the middle of my sleep. Yes, I reviewed things like threats when to exchange, or having a reasonable assessment with mobility. And, I've noticed other things since Kenneth_fiore_3 asked me if I considered alternatives. He's also said that other moves should've come into consideration. Plus, I've made comments in annotations, like I wouldn't have hesitated to play this. Or, I would've made that move without even giving it second thoughts. It boiled down to playing moves because they didn't just look good but seemed like it with one variation. The thing, however, is that I didn't look deeply into that variation or think of anything else that could make it fail. That was just me being impatient which affected my play, big time. And, in the recent training game, during my sleep, that attitude lost quickly which is exactly where I learned (after a problem I had at work) just how important it is to be patient in both chess and in life. And, I remembered that here, for I had an opportunity to win material, but rather then take his Rook, I remembered that important lesson about being patient. In doing so, I found some variations that didn't win material. Instead, it was even more, as that's how I wound up delivering a mate. So, how did it unfold?
