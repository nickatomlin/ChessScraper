I thought that a new tournament was going to begin last week. But, Mariann wanted a vote to see who wanted one to begin or who just wanted to play for fun. My guess is that she wasn't ready yet. But, it was a unanimous vote for the students to play for fun. And, there won't be one next week since this opponent can't make it then, so it'll likely start in two weeks, when everyone's there. The reason I wanted it held off was to be able to be able to do more OTB annotations. As noted, once it starts, the notations, and thus, annotations, will be suspended for a while. So, we know about a lot of the members in Jack Stockel's club including Tom Levine who has, on occassion, helped to coach other players like me. But, there is a Tom that you're not familiar with named Tom Walsh. I have played against him before, even in the last tournament. But, he's new here and is about as strong as I am. And, this is the first game against him that I get to annotate. In addition, the last game was interesting, but I did not notate because it was on the reverse side of the board as the letters were reverse. Meaning, that white's pieces were on the eighth rank, as opposed to, the first rank. In order for me to annotate, it's all got to be the right side. Anyway, after getting an overwhelming advantage, I did still played respectably well, as Tom did. But, I made one or two mistakes in the very end that lost the game. So, how did it all go down?
