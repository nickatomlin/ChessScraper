jkarp (1319) on 07-Jul-16:
No. That is an important concept. First, being a piece down is bad for you. Second, it's more then just that as you're down a piece and two pawns. Third, don't underestimate passed pawns. They're very dangerous, and one is often enough to win. I once made a mistake against Jack Stockel. No, I didn't blunder away a piece but was two pawns down. This enabled him to get a passed pawn which was enough to win. So, his advice was reminding me of not to give anything away since those passed pawns are important. As just one pawn, in it of itself, could make the difference between a win and loss for that reason. But, being down a piece on top of that is almost a guaranteed win for him. All white can do now is hope that black will make a mistake which would allow you a swindle, a draw or win in a lost game. However, it's important to note that chances are, that he won't because players with more experience generally don't let you make a comeback once they have a forced win, like this.
