I annotated my second game against Mariann, yesterday. But, this, however, was my first game against her. And, there was quite a bit of coaching. However, I had to do it differently from the way I coach on this website. The irony is that it was supposed to be a consultation game with Jack Stockel, but he got tied up against Bernard, and then, combined with Tom. So, I wound up coaching her, myself. Usually, I mention what could've been done differently after the mistake was made, so the best move right away. In the chess club, however, it does not work out that way. For, if I do too much coaching, Jack Stockel will, more then likely, change the positions. This means that the moves will be changed and annotations so that the recording will be messed up which will make it impossible to annotate after a certain point. The reason being is that he wants us to follow OTB rules. Now, unrated training games are not against GK rules. But, they're stricter in an OTB tourney. Therefore, a game chat is considered colluding during your games, and thus, cheating which would get you disqualified from the tournament. Even if that didn't happen where he gets involved, since Jack wasn't there for a week when I coached this same student, it's not just me and the opponent in that room. There are other people in the room, and I'm trying to be considerate of them, as Ardin previously mentioned. If there's too much noise, then they get distracted, and it's likely that one of them would make a terrible blunder. Now, I know what it's like to be distracted, and those were the days that I didn't enjoy since I played poorly. Last week was a perfect example. Therefore, I do most of the coaching after the second game, while saying to goodbye to each other and putting things away. But, it's where I spend the most amount of time going over where she went wrong. However, as noted in my last annotation, I was paired up against her, and this is where I was going to take the time to try and help her improve based on things that Jack might not have told her. So, she made a terrible mistake. And, that led to more mistakes which was going to lead to Rome, being mated. But, her first big blunder is what lost this game. So, how did it unfold?
