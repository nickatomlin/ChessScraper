archduke_piccolo (2206) on 13-Jan-17:
When situations are this hopeless, it doesn't do to be overcritical. Every move White has will lose. Here's a thing: some moves will make the game last longer than others; and some moves might make the stronger side work harder, or exercise more caution, or maintain a high level of alertness. Which policy (insofar as a policy is feasible) should the weaker side adopt? Personally, I would strive for the latter, even if it meant that, with accurate play by my opponent, the game would be shortened thereby. White does well to mobilize his undeveloped knight and free his rook, especially as Black was threatening to force a further exchange by 14...Bd5.
