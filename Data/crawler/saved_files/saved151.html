 ?! Thus, finding it a nuicense and ignoring the pressure, I decide to fianchetto my Bf8 at once by not thinking about anything else. In other words, nothing else came into consideration. But, this is an inaccuracy because will get a favorable exchange which puts me at a disadvantage. For one thing, he is exchanging, my N for his B. As with most position, the N is, in general, more favorable, as I learned from GM Susan Polgar. In fact, that's why when I asked her about exchanges, she said that as a general rule, you should exchange the N for B. And, she also stressed it in the mega bundle course that I purchased which is, "Polgar University 2017." This is everything that she is covering in the university where she is teaching chess. So, by ignoring what's occurring, Jack will be able to get the exchange that Susan has mentioned. Second, in the process, Mr. Stockel will be able to double up my pawns. And, what's worse is that I'll be forced to recapture away from the center which is another benefit for white. Better was to self impose the pin by advancing my e pawn, but then, Jack would still get the beneficial exchange. Therefore, best was to retreat my Nf6.

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 15-Mar-17:
The knight-bishop exchange is a whole tricky area. When moving a bishop to attack a knight as White has done, he should be fairly clear in his mind already what he will do if Black strikes (in this case) by ...h6. But also his planned response to other Black moves. After this pawn push, BxN comes into serious consideration. With 5.BxN, White gets something extra to sweeten the deal: a disruption in Black's structure. Is it worth it? Hard to say.
