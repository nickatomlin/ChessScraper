jkarp (1319) on 25-Sep-15:
Except that instead of taking, white is going to find the best move. So, instead of anticipating the wrong move, it's better to assume that your opponent will find the best response. That's because this is hope chess, as you're making a move in hope that your opponent will go wrong. More often then not, Hope Chess is costly. Better is Real Chess where you're actually playing and evaluating chess carefully. It's worth noting that Hope Chess and Real Chess are terms I learned from cplusplus11 and are extremely important in your games. Unless you're in a lost position and going for a swindle, if you go into the game or make a move thinking hmm, I'll play this and see where this goes because the opponent might go wrong, that's actually self destructive because, if he or she finds the right response, you might be worse off. And, that point, GM Susan Polgar emphasized, at the intro of her book, "Tactics for Champions", when discussing why attempting cheapo tactics is not a good idea.
