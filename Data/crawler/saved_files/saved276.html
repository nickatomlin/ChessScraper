Passed Pawns are not Everything
jkarp (1298) vs. henry10 (1281)
Annotated by: jkarp (1319)
Chess opening: Guatemala defence (B00)
Interactive ? Show all comments ? All annotated games ? View chessboard as:

Some time ago, Henry joined my club, Chess Coaches and Mentors. To be exact, it was right after the consultation between penguin and knightrider62 had ended (which started in Chicoboy's Legacy, as some will recall.) Later on, I applied and joined his club because his beliefs match up with mine. It has to do with teaching chess and helping players learn. The reason why is that we both believe that chess can and does help you in life, as noted in my profile. Since I saw that, I joined. And, once that happened, he challenged me to this game. It was interesting, and I won material. But, then, I did something which will not be done again. When Jack played Tom, Tom was up a Rook. However, he left which is common when he loses. After resigning, I told Jack that I think his opponent had a win. From there, I had a lot to learn. Sure, he was up an entire Rook, but Jack's pawns were not only passers, but they were about to promote. And, although Tom had one or two passed pawn and could advance, it was useless as Jack's pawns were going to promote with no way to stop them because he had a pawn chain, whereas, Tom did not. That's where he coached me, and I realized that there was much to learn. Therefore, I thought along the same line thinking. As I sacked pieces, actually the equivalent to when TA sacs his Q for pawn in his blitz games. Instead of defending myself, as noted in my second game with Ken, I decided to give him pieces. That's because with that lesson, I thought that a passed pawn was enough to win. But, I was wrong because, towards the end, he got my Rook and Queen. From there, I realize that there was nothing because one of them was behind the passer. Since my Queen was gone, and he was going to R there was nothing I could do with the passed pawn. Therefore, it became useless. That's when I realize that I was going to lose that, and from there, I resigned. So, how did this game go down?
