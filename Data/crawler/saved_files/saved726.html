jkarp (1319) on 15-Nov-15:
Although c5 is the most common, this is in, no shape or form bad. In on other words, it is a very good counter. 1.e4 is the King's Pawn Openings, but this response is the King's Pawn Game and it is a very common response. In fact, a lot of my students counter with this, and it can lead to some very interesting things like the Four Knight's Defense, I believe they call it Petrov, and there's also Ruy Lopez, Guaco Piano and the King's Gambit. Now, all of this starts with 1.e4, e5 Correction: This is actually most common. The reason I said that 1...c5 was is because that's the Siclian Defense which is the very first defense that my coach made me learn. However, I'm going to leave the comments above because the idea is that this, as well as, what you've mentioned are all very common openings. In fact, there's one that wasn't mentioned here which starts off with 1...c6 which is the Caro Kann defense, so what that means is that there's a variety to choose from with the KPO's.
cdeburca (1840) on 16-Nov-15:
This is grand - but you have to be aware of more sharp gambit lines than if you played c5 or e6. There's the King's Gambit, the Scotch Gambit, the Danish Gambit, the Evans Gambit, the Fried Liver and other lines. 1. e4 e5 can be quite a ride!
archduke_piccolo (2206) on 17-Nov-15:
Nothing wrong with 1...e5 at all. 1....c5 (Sicilian) is slightly more popular (not a lot in it), possibly because it is more of a counter-attacking line. My favourite for a long time has been 1...e6 (French Defence). Not as popular as the other two, it has the virtues of solidity, and long-term counter-attacking prospects. It does require some patience, though, at the highest levels.
