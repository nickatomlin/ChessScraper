kenneth_fiore_3 (2113) on 09-Aug-15:

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 10-Aug-15:
I think Joe meant 'diagonally', as the example he has given (f4, gxf3 e.p.) is correct. The 'en passant' capture is made AS IF the pawn moved one square forward instead of two. It also has to be emphasised that you get just one chance to make the capture against the pawn that has made the advance. Choose not to take, and the pawn makes it past the danger zone. Also note that 'en passant' captures can be made only on pawns that have made the two-square initial move. +++++++++++++++ Just a note on nomenclature: Diagonals are lines through corners of the squares. 'Orthogonals' is a word you don't often meet in chess, but I think it a useful one. It describes lines parallel to the edges of the board - up and down, or side to side. Orthogonals are divided into 'ranks' (side to side) and 'files' (up and down). Files are identified by their associated letter, so we might talk of the 'e-file', say. Ranks used to be identified, using the Descriptive Notation, by the the number according to the player's point of view. Hence the notion of the rook on the seventh. If Black had a rook standing on, say, c2, it would be on White's second, but Black's seventh rank. But using Algebriac, I don't know what the convention is. I am tending towards calling it (in this instance) the '2-rank' in the style similar to the way I identify files.
