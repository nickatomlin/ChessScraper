archduke_piccolo (2206) on 20-Jul-16:
The reason for Kenneth's comment, is that Black has by his play accepted an isolated centre (d-) pawn. By taking ...Nexd5, he would have left himself with a piece on the outpost square d5 - even in this line: 8...nexd5 9.Nxd5 Nxd5 10.Bxd5!? Qxd5. Having said that, I can see some strategic grounds for Black's choice, seeking a more substantial hold upon the centre. That centre pawn can act as cover for Black's preparation of a K-side attack, say.
