I enjoy annotating a game that has a lot of theory. The reason being is that studying theory is a way to become a better chess player. With that being said, this was an interesting game between tactical_abyss (Joe) and his opponent on another website. And, it is all theory since he notated this game in his club, "The Deeper Chess Theory." Now, I have studied quite a bit of his games, and applied them in three or four of my games to win, in what was a lost position. Plus, it's shorter as for now, that is until I'm off from work, I don't have time to annotate a long game, until I have a day off. And, as homework, I will be annotating some Joe's games since it involves theory. The idea is the same as when I annotated a game from a certain someone, it's the same idea that that theory is vital. So, with that being said, this for a short game was instructive and interesting between tactical_abyss and his opponent. In this game, like some of the other's that I have studied and one that I annotated, Joe sacced his Q early and was far behind, yet, he won in the opening. So, how did this game unfold?

 The moral to this story, as per what Jack Stockel and I annotated with anitam. Yes, C++ was right that there was a little more to it, but here; it especially applies here since TA implied it at his club which I absolutely agree with. That's due to one of Jack Stockel's annotation where a player lost to him for the same reason. "When you think that you have won or are in a winning a position, be very careful, and do not underestimate your opponent. Otherwise, you might start making mistakes and losing the game." To support it's important to note that many players, after having had won the Q early think that it's won. So, it doesn't matter what else they play, even weak moves. But, that is what seals their fate. The idea being is that just because you have the advantage doesn't mean that you should become overly confident. Instead, it's important to play the same way, as you would with any game. That's because becoming careless could cost you the game, as it did here.
