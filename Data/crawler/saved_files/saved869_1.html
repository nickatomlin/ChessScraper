archduke_piccolo (2206) on 16-Aug-15:
In the face of something unexpected, it is not a bad idea get up out of your chair, look at some other games going on - even think about how you might play the next move in some of them - and then return after a few minutes (if you have them to spare) to look at your own game with fresh eyes. Then you might have seen that after 24.Ba3! there was not a single safe square on the back tank for Black's King's Rook. Black would probably have had to try for complications by 24...Nc3! forking Bishop and Rook, but White can then continue, per spec, with 25.Bxf8 Nxd1 26.Rxd1 Kxf8 27.d8=Q Bxd8 28.Rxd8ch Rxd8 29.Nxd8. You can try different move orders to see whether Black comes out any better than this.
