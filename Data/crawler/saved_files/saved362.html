This was the first time that I played against the computer in several months. But, as always, I'm at the hardest level for practice. Playing against this level of opposition, to me, is not much different then playing against elyhim. The computer puts up a difficult fight. Therefore, the Wizard Chess program is similar to what we see with Fritz, Komodo, Stockfish, and other computer programs. So, no matter what opening you play the computer will win which is good for learning purposes. With that being GM Susan Polgar taught me about a different opening called the London System. Knowing that I'd lose, anyway, I decided to try against the computer on the most difficult level. Despite the loss and the opening that I'm not used to, I feel that I played quite well. In fact, I view my as better then average here.

I suggest, Joe, that you start with an intermediate level, and up the level by one if you find yourself consistently winning (say four out of five would be consistent enough).
