archduke_piccolo (2206) on 31-May-16:
The bad part here, is that Black's bishop after ...Bd6; a3 will have very little scope, and no 'anchorage' squares anywhere useful. I would expect the WK to start thinking about invading via the light squares on the Q-side. He may prepare that by crystallizing out the K-side situation first. For instance, if Black can be induced to weakening the light squares on this flank, a second front might be opened for White. On the whole I'd be inclined to leave things as they are, there, and bring the King over to the Queen side at once.

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 31-May-16:
It looks as though 34...Be7 might just have been good enough to hold. I have a feeling White has gone wrong in this little sequence, but it's hard to figure out how. Quite a few interesting concepts are shown here. Black's bishop looks very bad - almost no scope whatever. Yet the upside of 'bad bishops' reveals itself: it can be very handy in defence. Even though the light squares are weak, White has been left with no avenue of ingress on the K-side UNLESS he is prepared to sacrifice by f4. That does not look promising.
