OTB WTW Mentoring Game-"For Real This Time"
Jack vs. Joe (1314)
Annotated by: jkarp (1319)
Chess opening: Sicilian (B54)

This was an interesting game between Jack and me. BTW, the next tournament starts next week, so I will not be annotating another OTB game, for approximately, 5-10 weeks. So, during the weeks that it wasn't happening, I got to play 3 games against Ted. Then, I played a game against Perry. However, I really wanted another mentoring session. When this happens, the first person that I want is Jack Stockel because I've had more training games with then anyonelse, as I've been his student for 11 years. Plus, he does things differently then your average coach. In fact, his ideas are similar to what you see from TA in his blitz Q sacs, and that's what I like about him. In doing so, what Jack does involves a similar method to elyhim's money in the bank. As Homework, he deliberately makes mistakes, as it is a quiz to see if you can exploit them and win. Just like when Joe gives a handicap by giving Queen odds. If you win, he's proud. But, more often then not, the student loses, and that's when he gets in your thought process to explain what you're doing wrong. In my case, it was moving too quickly, so he tells me to slow down. I was, however, able to win the first game against him. But, it took help, and that's when he told me that I'm still moving too quickly. I agree because I also made terrible mistakes in the beginning that should've lost. For example, I blundered a piece, but then, he intentionally made a mistake that got everything back. Plus, I had exchanged way too often as I wasn't sure of what else to do, and as we know, one should not exchange just because it's possible to do so. But, that's what I did. And, it was back and forth mistakes until the very end. However, I almost lost the intiative and exchanged Rooks. But, that's when Jack told me that I was moving too quickly. So, the advice was to slow down and as a chess exercise, he told me to find the best move. The only reason for not playing it was that I was worried about losing the Rook. But, with the K confined to the corner, combined with doubled check, it was mate which I found. So, after winning that game, Jack implied the same thing that easy19 told me in the title which I called the annotation of, "For Real this Time." As Fred said, "This time, without lucky blunders or timeouts from my side, so brace yourself." Well, Jack implied the same thing by saying, "Are you ready? I helped you win the last game. But, this time, I am not going to help you. And, I am not going to make it easy for you." Sure enough, I made mistake which lost a piece and then another which lost at once. So, how did it all go down?

In this position, I was worried about losing the Rook, so I was about to exchange with Rxf5, black's last move was ...Rf5. Once I started playing that, he said, "study this position a little more. A couple of points here. 1. This advance is an important strategic decision that frequently crops up in Open Sicilian Defences.
