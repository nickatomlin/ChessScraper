archduke_piccolo (2206) on 06-Sep-15:
Black has played a Najdorf Sicilian with 5...a6, which is intended to prepare the way for 6...e5 if White will let him. White has played possibly the most aggressive response, which used to be the favourite at top levels 30 or 40 years ago. Also playable was Fischer's long-time favourite, the Sozin Attack (6.Bc5, which also tends to deter ...e5), or the more modern 6.Be3, which I think is associated with the name of the US GM Robert Byrne. Among a whole raft of other possibilities, 6.Be2 remains a fairly popular positional treatment. GM Magnus Carlsen played that line in the recent Sinquefield Cup Tournament in St Louis.
