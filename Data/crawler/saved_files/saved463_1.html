archduke_piccolo (2206) on 31-May-16:
This seems to me a reasonable move to consider, as although White gets a passed pawn it is isolated. I'd be very concerned, though, that it would be very difficult to attack on d5, as Black's bishop can not reach it, but White's can defend it. That would tend to leave the White knight with a free hand (once a rook gets on d1) to play to e4, reaching into the rear areas at f6 and d6. The 'bishops of opposite colour' can sometimes be an aid to a defender, but in different circumstances benefit the attacker. It has the effect of putting Black a piece ahead on the dark squares, and a piece behind on the light.
