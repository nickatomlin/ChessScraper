Some time ago, getoverit recommended that I studied games between stronger opposition. That started after we first met which occurred because of my annotations since I've received comments from damalfi and archduke_piccolo which getoverit studies. And, this is also why another tourney, Reversion's Open Fire, was started. And, that's why I was unavaliable for this tournament, as otherwise, I would've participated. The idea wasn't to annotate games that I played, but rather, what the others played. That's because by definition of the WTW club, they're all runners so I was learning by playing against it. But, it didn't follow WTW rules since the idea was that I'd resign once I felt that the position was lost. However, I didn't annotate much of those games because, aside from reversion and elyhim I didn't meet anyonelse for that tourney. But, this is special, as it was a very different tournament. This was in honor of therealjimfrior who passed away too soon. What's worth noting is that he was my mentor at WTWLTR club, when we were in separate mentoring groups. The idea was for the mentor to monitor the students progress as the founder, Joann, mentioned before it all became one group. And, he was a very good friend of charleshiggie, which is why Charles started this tourney. Plus, I took note of it because it happened in, "The Chess Problems Lovers" club. And, it's worth noting that one of the reasons make my annotations what they are (as to how I met reversion from that annotative style is because of Charles.) With that in mind, there is a special place in my clubs for his annotated games. And, Charles is my favorite annotator of all time because his notes have helped me to improve. Plus, they're useful for beginners. In fact, I felt that I was still in training after my last game with Ken when reading his recent annotations. Similar , Janet is the only other one that I've met in this tournament. With all of the special circumstances, I feel that it is, very much, worth annotating this game. As I believe that Charles played, as usual, extremely well. But, he was up against stronger opposition which made it possible for Janet to win, just like, in one of their games where there was 4 Queens, as Charles annotated, called, "4 Queens and a Mystery". So, how did it all go down?
