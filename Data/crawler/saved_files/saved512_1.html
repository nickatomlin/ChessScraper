cheapshotfail (1759) on 13-Apr-16:
Yeah, I think after allowing Nxe3 and now this inaccuracy, black should be solidly better here, with a pleasant game to follow. Either the computer is saying white is lost here (which I'm not sure about) or just a solid advantage. Black's development practically plays itself, each piece has at least one good square to look forward to, several come with tempo on the misplaced white pieces. Structurally black's pawn structure is less exposed than white's, the f4 and c4 pawns only serve to leave white's king in a gaping vacuum. You can already see the fragility of white's position in that there are already tactics for black and none for white. Strategically black has accomplished a lot out of the opening, and you might say that black is already strategically winning as white is going to have to sit and think up a serious plan to not be much worse. White's king is going to have a hard time finding a home while black can easily catch up in development with 13...Bb4+ 14. Kd1 (14. Kf2 Qc7 -/+) and 14...0-0. Just that simple line should suffice to leave black permanently better. I'm not sure whether a computer would say that this is an advantage for black (+/-) or just flat out winning (-+) but my spidey-senses say that with perfect play white should be able to find drawing chances (so -/+), but only should they be able to survive without too much material loss until the endgame.
