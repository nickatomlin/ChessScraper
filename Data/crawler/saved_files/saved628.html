jkarp (1319) on 09-Jan-16:
For me, one point would be all the reason. On two occassions, I lost the c pawn, but through the open variation by failing to play 5.Nc3. The mentor noted how important it was to access the position carefully. Sadly, I forgot that in a game against my OTB coach. But, from then on, I remembered, once and for all. As Jack would say, "Don't give things away" which is something that elyhim emphasized in our training session. The idea being is that one pawn can make all the difference, in an endgame which is something important to keep in mind at the opening. The reason being, as chesskingdom64 put it, "The openings must be used with the greatest care. What you do in the opening will affect you in the midgame; what you do in the midgame will affect you in the endgame; what you do in the endgame will affect the final results."
