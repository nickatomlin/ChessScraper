archduke_piccolo (2206) on 25-Jan-17:
This is one of my favourite moves for White in this position: the Saemisch Attack. It harbours some aggressive intentions, namely, an attack very similar in shape, and quite analogous, to the Yugoslav Attack against the Sicilian Dragon. The f3-pawn support the break g2-g4 and h2-h4 if White gets his way. There are several alternatives. Another favourite of mind is the Four Pawns Attack (5.f4), but 5.Nf3 is pretty standard, and 5.Bg5 is also a playable line. I once had the Kramer System (5.Nge2) played against me by a future IM, and, after a terrific battle, lost on time in a winning position.

archduke_piccolo (2206) on 27-Jan-17:
More than 40 years ago I first saw what follows in this game in a book 'Chess With The Masters' by Martin Beheim, with additions by Leonard Barden, published in 1969. The line (with a slightly different move order) appeared as a footnote to chapter on the famous 1934 encounter M. Euwe vs Em. Lasker, in which Black gave up his queen for rook and knight to obtain POSITIONAL, rather than tactical advantages. The game, B. Spassky vs D. Bronstein (Amsterdam, 1956) seems to be the 'stem' game for this line (though with a slightly different move order to reach this position). I long wanted to play the thing, but never would have believed I would ever get the chance. Well, now, here it is! I simply could not, in all conscience, pass it up.
