archduke_piccolo (2206) on 21-Nov-15:
Just for the sake of interest, I thought I would look further into 5.Nxe5. Can it be made into a viable gambit? Let's see: 5.Nxe5 Nxb5 (...Qe7 is not as good) 6.Nxf7! Kxf7 7.Qh5ch g6! (Forced! Any other move leads to Black giving back both pieces.) 8.Qxc5 c6 9.a5 Nc7. Black escapes with his extra piece for two pawns, for which White doesn't have enough attack. So, no: not a gambit. But it is sometimes worth looking at 'bad' moves, just to see where they lead. They can be quite illuminating.
