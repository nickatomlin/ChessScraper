archduke_piccolo (2206) on 14-Jan-17:
It is hard to see a plan behind these K-side advances, especially as White can scarcely contemplate castling on that wing, now. But it would take years to get castled on the other wing. White might as well have castled on move 6, and then try and untangle the Q-side by moving the bishop off d3. Black would have a devastating reply, here: 7...Nf4!!, and 7...Ndb4 is very nearly as powerful. E.g. 7...Nf4 8.Bf1 Nd3ch 9.Bxd3 ... {or else 9.Ke2 Bxf3ch 10.Kxf3 Qd5ch 11.Ke3 ... (Else Ke2; Qe4# or Kg4, Nxf2ch etc) 11...Bc4ch etc} 9...Qxd3 and White is going to lose the f3-knight, and, owing to the then potential mate on e2, White have to allow the queens to come off as well: 10.Qe2 Qf5 11.g4 Bxe2 12.gxf5 Bd3 and Black has a winning game.
