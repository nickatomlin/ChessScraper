kenneth_fiore_3 (2113) on 22-Oct-15:
It is called Sokolsky's Opening. I played against it vs, a local expert 2 games in which he played 1. b4 and I played 2 games against him with 1 b3. In all four games I won a pawn, but he was brilliant in defense, and all four games were drawn. I joked with him afterwards that he did prove that 1. b4 was one move better than I. b3. This was back in the 70's

The key to blitz is the element of surprise. Often you see obscure lines/moves from those who do well just to see how the opponent will react. More often than not, I even employ such an strategy just to see if I can psychologically beat my opponents. With that said, I rarely see things like this in OTB play and it's usually the masters/senior masters I've run into who play it.
