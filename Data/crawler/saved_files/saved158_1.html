archduke_piccolo (2206) on 13-Mar-17:
One of the reasons for the knight's going to g4, was to switch to the e5-square and induce an exchange. I do think White ought to have gone along with it (18.Nxe5) but I can understand the motivation to avoid exchanges. Part of the art of exchanging is knowing when to offer, when to accept; when to avoid, when to decline. In my view Nh2 was justified if White had a plan to redeploy, e.g. via f1 to e3 or g3, say. When one side has a material edge, he can use the threat of exchanges - of pieces particularly - to drive the opponent's pieces into increasingly remote locations.
