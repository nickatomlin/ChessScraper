archduke_piccolo (2206) on 02-Apr-16:
This has the look of two pugilists standing toe to toe in the middle of the ring raining blows upon each other. But White has allowed Black into the game somehow. This pawn thrust looks to recover the sacrificed piece, but Black had a fine alternative that added interest to her investment: 24...Qe3ch! White has two options: [A] move the King, or [B] interpose the rook (leaving it pinned). [A] 25.Kh2 ... (It actually doesn't matter what K-move he plays, it amounts to precisely the same thing), 25...Qg3ch 26.Kh1 Qxh3ch 27.Kg1 Qg3ch 28.Kh1 Rf6, and checkmate by ...Rh6ch looks unstoppable. So, [B] 25.Rf2 Bh4 Pinned against the King the Rook is immobile. Note that the Queen is also attacking the unprotected bishop at a3. But rather than snip it off straight away, exploits the pin of the rook. If White plays, say, 26.Qe1 then 26....Bxf2ch 27.Qxf2 Qxa3. Or, if 26.Bxe3 Qxf2ch 27.Kh1 Bg3 28.Qg1 ... (the only way to stop the mate) 28...Qxg1ch 29.Rxg1 Rfd8 and, the Exchange and two pawns tio the good, Black should win without much trouble.
