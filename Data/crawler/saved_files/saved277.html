 He puts his Rf8 onto its most logical square, controlling the semi-open e file. In chess, a half-open file (or semi-open file) is a file with only pawns of one color. The half-open file can provide a line of attack for a player's rook or queen. A half-open file is exploited by the player with no pawns on it.
1 comment
jkarp (1319) on 23-Nov-16:

And, one of them was how useful those semi open files are because getting the Rook or Queen involved. In fact, in one of the games which I annotated, despite being down Queen for pawn in the opening, Joe won. That's because his opponents mistake was letting TA get that semi open file. So, how useful they are is a well known fact, especially because before that, it was one way in which GM Judit Polgar beat Anand, before he became a world champion.
