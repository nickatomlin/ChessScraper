archduke_piccolo (2206) on 15-Aug-15:
I had to look up 'priyome' (pronounced pree-yom). In the context of C++11's comment, it would seem to be cognate with 'guideline' or 'standard approach', rather than 'method' or 'technique'. As such it behoves the player to determine whether the chessboard situation differs sufficiently from usual to justify departing from the standard approach to it. In the Sicilian, Bc4 by White has the downside of inducing ...e6 whereat the bishop is said to be 'biting on granite.' So it is usually avoided. But there are some lines in which it is quite strong, and by inducing ...e6, obviates ...e5. This leads us, for example, to the Sozin Attack against the Sicilian Defence, a long-time favourite of Bobby Fischer.
