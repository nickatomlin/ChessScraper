charleshiggie (2038) on 30-Apr-17:
The idea is that after checking on d8 and then d7 white takes the pawn on c7. White can then hold onto one of his queen-side pawns, but after 32. Rd8+ Kg7 33. Rd7+ Kg6 34. Rxc7 Rc2. 35. Rxb7 Rxc3 36. Rxb6+ Kg5 white has both the c4 and g4 pawns attacked and black can probably still draw.
