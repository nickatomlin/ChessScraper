I annotated Charles last game against Janet during the same tourney. As noted, these are the only two players that I know, in that tournament. In the last game, Janet, as expected won the game. But, it was after only one mistake from Charles which is how chess usually is. But, similar to a tourney involving the Traxler Defense, I don't think any of these players made a mistake. Yet, one still outplayed the other. So, in this case, Charles won. What makes this remarkable, just like the other game that I annotated is that Janet is a much stronger player. So, how did it all go down?

 But, I was surprised to see Janet take here. Yes, black is a pawn up for now, but those are doubled pawns. Of course, it doesn't matter now, but were I black, I wouldn't want to face this, keeping in mind that this could become a problem later on. And, that is something that tactical-abyss has emphasized when it comes to doubled pawns. He said that it all depends at what stage of the game you're at, more often then not. Early on, it usually isn't a problem, but that could change as you get later in the game.
