 Let us begin with a Philidor Defence. Fairly popular in the 19th Century, following the teachings of one Francois-Andre Philidor, it fell under a shadow during the 20th. It seems to have undergone something of a revival - if a minor one - in recent decades. It is an opening, I think, for the careful, solid player: patient, playing the cards close to the chest. Not an opening I have ever played as Black!
