I just learned something new. I always thought that a bad bishop was a bishop that could not developed on a good square, for a similar reason. But, if you had to cram your B as white on the second rank or black's on the seventh rank, so it could not go far from it's home square. Now, I learned that a bad bishop is a bishop blocked by it's own pawns, and a good bishop is one that it's not. An important since I was getting it wrong, and has, occasionally caused me to evaluate wrongly, and thus, making the wrong move. The reason being is that I was, subsconsiously, eliminating my good bishop, when I thought I had gotten rid of my bad bishop, during exchange variations.
