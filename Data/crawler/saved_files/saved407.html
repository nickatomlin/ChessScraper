This was an interesting game between Sandre and Joe. As I recently posted in my profile, tactical-abyss is an important reason that I've improved. By this, I don't mean recently but overtime (over the years that I have been on.) And, that I've used some of his methods to teach my students. I'm not alluding to irregular openings or the Q sacs because Joe didn't recommend it either. The only exception to that was my decision to play a double fianchetto against the KID against a student which I underestimated how bad that move really was. Instead, it was things like not being overly confident or overestimating the opponent when you think you've won. But, I made that connection by studying those things (Q sacs, irregular openings where he goes out of book.) As that's what also helped me understand where I wrong against anitam which after that loss, I was down to one of my lowest ratings of only 869. Most of this, however, is with blitz games. This, however, was not a blitz game. Yet, Joe still played irregular openings and did not develop anything until much later on. This left Sandre much better development and a lead in center control, but Joe still won this game. How did he do it?
