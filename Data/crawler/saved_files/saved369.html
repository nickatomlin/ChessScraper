Another 4 Q's variation
Susan Polgar vs. Vladimir Dimitrov

This was an interesting game between GM's Susan Polgar and Vladimir Dimitrov. In fact, it was one of the most notable games. Back in the day, I was given Homework on certain days while receiving mentoring. Since I've started doing my own studies, I call the shots and pick the day to those assignments. I decided that days like today are ideal and am planning to do two or three. The reason being is that I'm off, and my sleep schedule was thrown off a bit because of the night shifts in which I wasn't ready until about midnight local time. I used to need monster drinks but have settled with energy (awake chocolate bars) to stay awake on the job. But, I was thinking that instead of needing that (especially with Hot and Humid weather where water is best), when it comes to chess, there is something that tires me out and makes me ready to sleep at night even if I'm not used to a specific schedule. It's games like this in which I have a difficult time figuring out why certain moves were played since it's a GM played (where they see things that we don't.) And, I also kept in mind that it's a good way to become a better chess player. Not to mention that I have taken a couple of course from Susan Polgar. So, I feel that this is a good way to go. In addition, charleshiggie annotated a 4 Q's and mystery game against brigadecommander, in which, she won. In that annotation, though, I mentioned about this game where there was game chat of a 4 Q's game between her and Charles. I later applied that to force a win against redfoxrising who's rating is close to 2000. Even though it wasn't 4 Q's, I used the same concept to promote which allowed 3 Q's. So, I had the extra Queen to deliver a checkmate. Well, here is another example where both players promote while having their Queens leaving with a 4 Queen's battle for a couple of moves until one gets traded off. However, Susan won this game. So, how did she do it.

Unlike with the game against Ken, it worked, so I felt that I learned a bit from my mistake on Friday. Therefore, I'm applying a similar concept here with Susan, except that this is not Hope Chess.
