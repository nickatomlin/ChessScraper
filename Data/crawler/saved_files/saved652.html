This was my first Wizzard Chess Game in a long while. The last time I played this was back in February (when I was considered to have left this website.) Since then, I felt that it took away too much time from enjoying GK (which doesn't happen, in reality.) As noted then, Wizzard Chess is a computer program. It started off with the First Harry Potter Movie. So, the difference between that and standard chess is that, when you move a pawn or piece, it's like your moving them alive. In other words, when they move, you're seeing the magic so that the Knight flies, the Bishop comes out with a sword. The Rook is like a Wizzard or Troll (Depending on which your color you are), the Queen for white represents Harminy, and the King represents Dumbledore. If you're black, however, the Queen represents an evil helper of Valdimort that's in love with him (forgot the name), and the monarch represents his evil snake. The pawns are the soilders (both good and evil). On the computer, they're very good. And, the idea behind playing this is to get better, so it is the equivalent to a training game. However, it's indirect since I'm up against the computer, which means there's no coaching or mentoring. That is, unless you picked another player near you (which doesn't happen). With that being said, it's not considered a WTW Game, since one is not up against a runner. But, you are learning how to run (LTR) since it's about improving. Therefore, it's meant for that, and given that I've won on the easiest level and drawn on the intermediate scale, as Homework, I picked the most difficult level. Against something like this, it's equal to being up against 2400+ rated player. In fact, I feel like I'm playing against a Senior Master, or even, a GM. Now, I'm used to playing as white, but since I have not done it with the color, I've decided to play as black, this time around. And, that's because, this way, there's more to learn. With a terrible blunder, I lost in under 20 moves. So, how did this game unfold?
