As I mentioned to reversion, I like mixing in matching when it comes to annotating games. Specifically, I enjoy lectures presented by tactical-abyss because there's a lot to learn. In fact, I've learned more from him the one could imagine. Mind you that each lecture is an equivalent, and sometimes, better then an annotation. Now, I have annotated a lot of his Queens sacs, but with what he teaches, I enjoy mixing and matching. Specifically, it's his blitz games where Joe plays unusual, and even irregular openings. A lot of times, he does not follow General Opening Guidelines and still wins. Might not seem so, but for me, this is significant. For one thing, not only can't his opponent get away with deviating, but he or she must play accurately to keep their advantage. Like many other of Joe's blitz games, tlafleur made some mistakes and lost. So, how did this particular game unfold?
