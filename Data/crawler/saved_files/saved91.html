Well, there is nothing wrong with the opening. But, the way you're thinking is not good here. I do understand it though, as that's how I used to think. In doing so, with the same process, I kept trying different openings and for the same reason. In fact, that's one reason why I desperately needed coaching several years ago. And, my attitude didn't change. The thing that made the difference was GM Susan Polgar. She implied that we shouldn't try something different just because the opponent has a lot of experience with the opening played. So, it's not what's most analyzed by masters and grandmasters. Instead, her recommendation was to play an opening that you have the most experience with. That is, even if, the opponent has as much. For as she put it, "there is no such thing as a won opening." So, keep this in mind, for the future. The idea isn't how much experience they have, but rather, how much experience you have with the opening, Edna.
